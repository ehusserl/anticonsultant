Cornell Redesign Blog

We did a risky thing at Cornell, but we had to take a chance. The university sorely needed a redesign of its web space. It had to be done in months. And it had to be embraced by the community.

Knowing that to be embraced we needed to embrace, we decided to open up the process. Total transparency—to 150,000 alums—to deans and associate deans—to students and prospective students. We knew we couldn't possibly tailor communication to each interest group. So we created a blog.

We blogged about timelines, decisions, and strategy. We put fear aside and showed early designs. And then came the moment. The moment when the social media disease reared its head: frustrated anonymity. It allowed people to participate in the discussion behind a veil of indecipherable username. And it allowed them to take swipes at the team and the success of the project.

This was the blog entry that turned the tide.
